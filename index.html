Abstract.

The purpose of this work is to investigate the Influence of Manufacturing Parameters on Residual Stresses in Carbon-Epoxy Fiber Tubes manufactured by Filamentary Winding. The research carried out consists of a conceptual description of the influence of Residual Stresses on the performance of materials in general and composite materials in particular, together with the existing experimental techniques to determine Residual Stresses. First, a Design of Experiments was carried out to reduce the number of tests. This is determined taking into account that, although a large number of process parameters influence the manufacture of composite tubes, the equipment and facilities of our own and third parties do not allow a large number of tests to be carried out. This entails carefully selecting the experimental techniques for evaluating residual stresses, the number of tubes to be tested and the most appropriate factors to measure. In the theoretical framework part of this work, some predictive theoretical models of Residual Stresses were taken into account and hypotheses were postulated about the influence of manufacturing parameters on the Residual Stresses obtained.

As for the experimental part, first the tubes were manufactured using the Filamentary Coiling technique, the parameters were varied in each case, and the tubes were cured in an autoclave. Subsequently, measurements are made with the already manufactured tubes. Residual Stresses are measured using the Split Method. Mechanical Strength tests are also carried out (using ASTM D 2290) in order to obtain not only the magnitude of the Residual Stresses, but also their relevance, that is, how much it affects the structural resistance in the tubes. A micrographic analysis was performed to obtain Fiber Percentages, Resin Percentages and Voids (also called pores or cavities). Finally, the hypotheses were contrasted with theoretical models and experimental values.
